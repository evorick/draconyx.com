About Rick

I have always been interested in computers since 1994, as a kid installing games through the command line on my Windows 3.1 computer. However, I have never thought seriously about pursuing a career in programming until much later.

I had attended Rutgers University in New Jersey for a double major of Evolutionary Anthropology and a History/Political Science joint major. After that I attended a Master's Degree program at Colorado State University for Biological Anthropology. After graduate school I worked for a couple years as a manager at Cintas Corporation, the largest business service provider which employs around 30,000 people. I am grateful for the tremendous amount of business, management, and interpersonal experience I gained while working for Cintas. I was successful with Cintas but I ended up craving a career that contained different mental challenges.

Everything changed after I had attended the Startup Weekend in Colorado Springs, CO in the fall of 2013. The whole atmosphere of startups and the tech industry in general had fascinated me. For a couple of months I had attempted a startup concept only to realize that it would not be possible without considerable programming skills. I realized that I needed to “bite the bullet” and learn to code. Then I started to learn the basics of programming with online resources such as, Codecademy, Udacity, and Team Treehouse. Those classes were the catalyst for me craving a deeper understanding of computer programming.

I became even more interested after my time spent at Startup.SC ( a Myrtle Beach, SC technology incubator). I started in the space by working through some JavaScript lessons and eventually ended up being the business development officer for the tech startup LunaDesk (part of Startup.SC’s first cohort). That was great because I was further embedded into the startup world, but it also de-facto ended my progression with learning to code.

After my time spent at LunaDesk, I realized that I still enjoyed the challenge of coding and everything I learned about it. I was not ready to give up on programming and continued my online lessons while working along with A Simpler Story by helping customize the HTML and CSS of Wordpress sites. I ended up participating in a two-week Tech Talent South pop-up code school in Myrtle Beach in June 2015. I am currently attending the Tech Talent South full-time Code Immersion Program which covers HTML/CSS, Ruby, Ruby on Rails, domain modeling, RESTful design, JavaScript and AJAX, Git and Github, and understanding and utilizing API’s.

I love that with coding you are like an all powerful wizard that can create something with just your fingers. It is also a career that necessitates lifelong learning, I really like that.
